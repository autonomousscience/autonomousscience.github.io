AISLE network architecture

The AISLE architecture defines how autonomous labs exchange data, reasoning, and experiments across institutions. It unifies instrumentation, intelligent data management, and agentic decisioning through a shared data fabric.

  • Instrument integration
    Standardized interfaces that let agents control heterogeneous lab assets.
  • AI decision loops
    Models and agents that adapt experiments based on live observations.
  • Interoperable protocols
    Secure, cross-lab communication for coordinated workflows.

Four pillars of autonomous science

Each pillar advances the shared capability for scalable, trusted, and rapid discovery.

Autonomous instrumentation

Vendor-agnostic control, calibration, and orchestration across robotic platforms.

Agentic intelligence

AI agents plan, reason, and optimize experiments in collaboration with scientists.

HPC + digital twins

Simulation, surrogate models, and twin ecosystems tighten the decision loop.

Trust + provenance

FAIR, auditable, and policy-aware pipelines enable reproducible autonomous science.
